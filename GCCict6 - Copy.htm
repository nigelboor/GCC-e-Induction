Legislation

Within the Council we have a duty to keep information secure, but must balance that with transparency and openness. There are two key pieces of legislation that you must be aware of when using, collecting, storing, sharing, reading, or doing anything with information.

Data Protection

This is a law put in place to protect people’s information (personal data)

Personal data is any data relating to a living individual which identifies them, e.g. name, address, data of birth, national insurance number. It includes an expression of opinion about the individual and any indication of the intentions of the organisation.

Some information is more sensitive than others, e.g. information about an individual’s:

mental or physical health, political beliefs, religious beliefs, sexual life, membership of a trade union, racial or ethnic origin, criminal offences (including alleged),

There are various rules around the use of personal data, called the 8 principles.

8 Principles

  1. Fair and lawful
    Make sure people know why their information is being collected, what it will be used for and who it will be shared with.
    We must work within our powers.
    Do not use personal information for anything that will break a law.
    Must comply with the conditions in the Act, e.g. you have consent to use the personal data, or there is a legal obligation to do something.
  2. Specific purpose
    Only use the information for the reasons it was collected for.
  3. Adequate and not excessive
    Make sure you have enough information to do the task, but don’t collect information ‘just in case’.
  4. Accurate
    Make sure the information is correct.
    If it is an opinion, make sure that this is clear.
  5. Retained for as long as necessary
    Only keep information as long as needed, either by law or for business need.
    Keep information in-line with the retention schedules.
    Use the confidential facilities to destroy information.
  6. Individual’s rights
    The public and staff have the right to access the personal information held about them, including in emails.
    People have the right to claim compensation for damage and distress.
    People have the right to request use of their personal data to cease.
    People have the right to stop the use of their personal data for marketing purposes.
  7. Security
    Keep personal information secure at all times, whether electronic or manual.
    Make sure Data Protection is in agreements with contractors.
  8. Transfer out of EEA
    Be careful what you put on the Internet/website.
    Contact the Information Management Service if you need to send personal information abroad.

Consequences of getting it wrong

  • To an individual – harm or distress, danger, identity fraud.
  • To an employee – extra work, disciplinary proceedings, dismissal, criminal prosecution, prison, fines.
  • To the Council – complaints, criminal prosecution, damaging publicity, unlimited compensation claims, fines of up to £500,000.
