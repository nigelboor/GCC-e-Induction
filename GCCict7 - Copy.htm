What is Freedom of Information (FOI)?

  • The public’s general right of access to recorded information
    – Electronic, Paper, Email, Video etc
  • Right to know what information is held
  • Right to have the information communicated to them
  • The emphasis is on disclosure
  • Put in place to find out how public money is spent and how/why decisions are taken
  • It is about improving openness, transparency and accountability in public bodies

What does this mean to us?

  • Produce and maintain a publication scheme
  • Manage our information
  • Only write things down that are accurate
  • Never write anything down that you wouldn’t want released to the public
  • Recognise requests for information
  • Assist people who are thinking of or are trying to make an FOI request
  • Respond to requests within 20 working days
  • Have a complaints/review procedure

What is a request?

  • Any written request for recorded information, e.g. email, fax or letter
  • Must describe the required information
  • Must provide a name and contact details, e.g. email address
  • Can be from anyone, anywhere in the world
  • There is no need to mention FOI Act
  • A request should be taken on face value and the reasons behind the request should not affect our response (motive and applicant blind)

If you get something which you think could be an FOI request forward it to foi@gloucestershire.gov.uk or phone 01452 425071 or 01452 42 5186.
