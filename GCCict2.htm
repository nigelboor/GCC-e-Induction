How to choose a good password

ICT Security is everyone’s responsibility. GCC has a duty to ensure that the information it handles is kept safe. Choosing a good password is like choosing a good lock on your front door at home and this is down to each individual user to follow the basic steps below:

  • Passwords should be at least 8 characters long.
  • Utilise both letters and numbers, i.e. alphanumeric.
  • Use upper and lower cases.
  • Avoid using commonly known facts about yourself, e.g. name of your partner, date of birth, car registration number.
  • Try not to use words found in the dictionary. Hackers tend to use a dictionary file to crack passwords.

Just like your front door key, please do take care of your password.

Don’t write it down where it can be easily interpreted by others.

Do not give your password to anyone else – not even the Support desk staff who are trying to help!

General password advice can be found in the downloads section of this page on Staffnet.
