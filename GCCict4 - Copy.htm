Protective Marking

In order to be able to understand the level of security that is required for different types of information you should protectively mark your documents. The council holds information that falls into 3 protective marking categories:

UNCLASSIFIED

This is used to indicate positively that a protective marking is not needed. Information would fall into this category if:

  • release wouldn’t cause any damage or distress to individuals,
  • if it is freely available in the council’s publication scheme or website; or
  • no Freedom of Information (FOI) or Environmental Information Regulations (EIR) exemptions apply to the information

PROTECT

This is used to indicate that a level of security is required. Information would fall into this category if released would:

  • cause distress or potential harm to individuals; breach proper undertakings to maintain the confidence of information provided by third parties; breach statutory restrictions on the disclosure of information
  • cause financial loss or loss of earning potential, or facilitate improper gain;
  • unfair advantage for individuals or companies;
  • prejudice the investigation or facilitate the commission of crime;
  • disadvantage government in commercial or policy negotiations with others.

RESTRICTED

This is used to indicate that a high level of security is required. Information would fall into this category if released would:

  • affect diplomatic relations adversely;
  • cause substantial distress to individuals;
  • cause financial loss or loss of earning potential or to facilitate improper gain or advantage for individuals or companies;
  • prejudice the investigation or facilitate the commission of crime;
  • breach proper undertakings to maintain the confidence of information provided by third parties;
  • impede the effective development or operation of government policies;
  • breach statutory restrictions on disclosure of information;
  • disadvantage government in commercial or policy negotiations with others
  • undermine the proper management of the public sector and its operations.
